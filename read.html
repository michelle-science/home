Featured Readings

The Water Cycle

The water cycle describes how water moves above, on, and below the surface of the Earth. Water changes states between liquid, vapor, and ice at various places in the water cycle. Learn more at USGS Water Science School.

Introduction to Ecosystems

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms and their nonliving environment. Ecosystems can be as large as a desert or as small as a puddle. Read more at National Geographic: Ecosystem.

What Is Energy?

Energy is the ability to do work. It exists in many forms such as heat, light, chemical, and electrical energy. Check out Energy.gov: Energy Sources for more information.

Atoms and Elements

Atoms are the basic building blocks of matter. Elements are pure substances made of one kind of atom. Learn the basics at LibreTexts Chemistry.

Photosynthesis Explained

Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert sunlight into chemical energy. Discover how it works at Khan Academy: Photosynthesis.

Gravity: The Force That Shapes the Universe

Gravity is a fundamental force that attracts objects with mass toward each other. Learn more about gravity at NASA Knows Gravity.

The Structure of the Cell

Cells are the basic units of life. This article explains the parts of animal and plant cells and their functions. See details at Cells Alive.

Understanding Ecosystems and Biomes

Explore the different types of biomes and how ecosystems function within them. Read more at National Geographic Habitats.

Electricity Basics

Electricity powers our world. Learn about electric current, circuits, and how electricity works at Energy.gov Electricity Basics.

Introduction to Forces and Motion

Forces cause changes in motion. This reading covers Newton’s laws and how forces affect objects. See more at The Physics Classroom.

Stars and Their Life Cycle

Stars go through stages from birth to death. Learn about star formation, main sequence, and supernovae at NASA Imagine the Universe.

Galaxies: Islands in the Universe

Galaxies contain billions of stars. Discover different galaxy types and their features at Space.com Galaxies.

Earth’s Atmosphere

The atmosphere protects life and regulates climate. Learn about its layers and composition at NASA Climate.

Understanding Orbits

Orbits keep planets and satellites moving around larger bodies. Explore orbital mechanics at NASA Knows: Orbits.

Biomes of the World

Biomes are large ecological areas with distinct climates and species. Get an overview at National Geographic Biomes.

Primary Succession

Primary succession is the natural growth of life in a new or barren environment. Learn about this ecological process at National Geographic Succession.

Secondary Succession

Secondary succession occurs after an ecosystem is disturbed but soil remains. Explore examples and stages at Britannica Ecological Succession.

Energy Transformations

Energy can change forms but is never lost. Learn about transformations like potential to kinetic energy at Khan Academy Energy Transformations.

States of Matter

Matter exists as solids, liquids, gases, and plasma. Understand their properties and changes at Chem4Kids Matter.

Introduction to the Solar System

Our solar system includes the Sun, planets, moons, and other objects. Explore it at NASA Solar System Exploration.
